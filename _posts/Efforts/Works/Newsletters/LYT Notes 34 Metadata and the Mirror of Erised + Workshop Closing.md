Metadata gives you information about the note you are looking at.

It does two major things:

1. Metadata can provide fast insights about the note
2. Metadata can be leveraged powerfully with queries

Metadata has a lot of potential. And that's why you must exercise caution.

Because the lure of metadata is powerful.

Just like the Mirror of Erised, you can get lost in it.

The types of metadata you can add to the top (or bottom) of a note can be overwhelming. The trap is giving yourself unnecessary busywork every time to create a new note. For the sake of your best thinking, you must avoid this.

**So instead of desiring perfect structure, let's focus on practical structure.**

Try to limit the amount metadata labels you use.

* Fewer metadata labels = easier to upkeep
* Fewer metadata labels = less cognitive load
